# OPSTA: A shiny-based online platform for statistical analysis of sports science theses

With the expansion of postgraduate enrollment in sports science in China, the challenges in research design and statistical analysis in master's theses have become increasingly evident. Many postgraduate students lack adequate statistical training, which often leads to inappropriate research designs, improper statistical methods, failure to check underlying assumptions, and inadequate reporting of effect sizes and confidence intervals. These issues compromise the scientific rigor and credibility of research conclusions. To address these challenges, this study introduces OPSTA (Online Platform for Sports Thesis Analysis), a user-friendly data analysis tool designed specifically for the sports science domain. Built on the Shiny framework, OPSTA integrates commonly used statistical procedures in sports science research, including descriptive statistics, independent-samples and paired-samples t-tests, one-way and multifactor analysis of variance (ANOVA), correlation analysis, regression analysis, and reliability and validity testing. The platform provides an interactive and visualized interface that guides users through data import, method selection, and result export. By lowering the barrier to statistical analysis, OPSTA emphasizes assumption checking and standardized result presentation, thereby reducing the misuse of statistical methods and enhancing the quality of reporting. The platform is available at https://menglab.org.cn/opsta/.
